Mentoring Application

Time

Sep 2020 - Nov 2020

Role

UX / UI design

Team

Individual project

Overview

The Guider application is a personal project and it's designed to easily enable people to find their mentors or mentees to get support and opportunities. I understood pain points from the mentees' side and mentors’ side in order to make a better mentoring service.

View final design
Context

It began with my personal experience. As a non-design background graduate as well as an Asian woman, I felt frustrated because of a lack of connection and ambiguity of direction. Plus, I’ve been always supporting gender equality and thinking about how to make a better environment for people like me. So I decided to design for support.

Problem & Insight

"Why is the gender gap still big in the tech industry? How can we close the gender gap and bring diversity? "

Research

1. Well..does the gender gap still exist?

Some might think they don’t see the gender gap anymore. But, According to statistics,


In 2020, only 25% of GAFAM’s employees are female.

The ratio of men to women in engineering is 5:1.

25.5% of Google’s leaders on a global scale are women.

2. Is it important to close the gap?

Diversity is going to bring benefits not only to individuals but also to corporations. It relates to productivity, creativity. Above all, it’s obvious that we fight against any discrimination against gender or race.

3. So what’s the reason? Why do we see fewer women there?

Hypotheses & Insight

I focused on "the lack of visible role models". And my suggestion is empowering minorities and women by mentoring.

Back up research

According to research...

Mentoring programs boosted minority representation at the management level from 9% to 24%

Women are more likely to have a mentor than men – 54% vs 48%

Painpoints of
current mentoring system

"Hard to find the right mentors or mentees"

Information Architecture
Sketch
1

Onboarding

Setting the user's interest

According to research, people found it hard to find the right mentees and mentors. That makes it difficult to keep a bond between them. So I suggest a matching system based on their interest at the onboarding stage.

2

Quick Search for Mentors

Mentors sorted by categories

The main feed is sorted by different categories such as suggested mentors, organization, top mentors, and a location where mentees are. Further, they can sort mentors by languages, time availability, goals of mentoring with a filter feature. These are essential for getting everyone opportunities no matter where mentees are.

3

Clear scheduling & requesting

Schedule cards and goal setting

According to my research, the biggest challenges of mentorship are “scheduling meetings” and “unclear goals” It’s revealed that those cause frustration to both mentors and mentees. So I tried to solve these problems at the requesting stage.

4

Remote meetings

Video Chat

In many situations, mentees and mentors are not available for onsite meetings. To solve this problem, remote meetings should be easy to access. Having a video chat feature, users’ don’t need to use other softwares for video chat.

5

Dashboard

Managing mentorship

According to research, mentees said they feel a big frustration when they haven’t got replies from mentors for requesting. And from a mentors’ side, they feel frustrated when they are not clear about mentees’ goal and it creates unexpected results. I suggest a dashboard section to manage mentees’ goals, schedules, situations of requesting. I believe this could encourage users to keep up with mentoring.
